About our animal shelter & services

We are a municipal animal shelter and control facility located at 215 W Market St in New Albany, In. We work closely with local non-profits and community organizations to reduce pet overpopulation and provide humane education programs to our community. We are open for receiving lost or surrendered pets Monday through Friday from 9am to 4:30pm, and Saturdays from 11am to 2pm. Adoption hours are weekdays from 12-4pm, and Saturdays, 11-2:30pm.

History

New Albany Floyd County Animal Control &Shelter was established by an inter-local agreement between the City of New Albany, Indiana and Floyd County, Indiana Governments in 1968. We provide services which safeguard public health and safety, educate its citizens on responsible pet ownership, and to provide placement for the animals in its care.
